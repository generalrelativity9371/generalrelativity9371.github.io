We are Team No. 9371

We're an NYC-based FTC Robotics team associated with The Dalton School. We compete in NYC and abroad, and are excited for the 2022-2023 season!

About Us

Our Chassis

We built a custom omni chassis to optimize speed, with multiple levels for attachments. We used delrin so we could easily change and adjust our mechanics such as spinner and draw slides, while the final level is a custom, aluminum plate that we CNC-milled. The aluminum gives extra strength and stability to the base, which is important for any fast moving robot.

Our Intake

Our intake utilizes a claw that we have iterated several times to intake cones in multiple orientations. This claw uses grip tape to provide enough friction between the cone and claw. To maneuver our claw, we utilize drawer slides allowing us to score on the highest junctions. The slides connect to our claw through a servo that functions as a "wrist mechanism" that was developed with generative design.

Our Software

Our software team has focused on making our code modular to prevent unneeded repetition. Each mechanism has a unique class that are then grouped into a larger hardware class, meaning tele-op and autonomous code can use any mechanism easily. Our auto program uses a camera to detect the color of our sleeve and then move into the correct parking space. Our tele-op specifically allows the driver to move relative to the robot or relative to the driver, allowing for easier driver control on the field.
